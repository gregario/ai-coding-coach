# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Solo developers who use AI coding tools (Claude Code, Copilot, Cursor) daily. They open this dashboard after a session or at the end of the week — same moment as checking a Strava run summary or closing their Apple Watch rings. Mood: curious, slightly competitive with themselves, wanting to improve. Context: terminal-adjacent, just finished a deep coding session, probably dark-mode environment.

Future: team leads reviewing aggregate progress. But v1 is personal.

## Product Purpose

Score your AI coding sessions and coach you to get better. Reduce wasted AI credits by identifying the habits that cost money without producing results. The dashboard is the "post-run summary" — what went well, what to focus on next time, how you're trending.

Success: the user opens it after every session. It takes 10 seconds to get the insight. They close it knowing exactly what to try differently next time.

## Brand Personality

Playful, motivating, athletic.

Like a personal running coach's debrief — honest about what was weak, celebratory about progress, never condescending. Uses the language of effort and improvement, not corporate metrics. "Your verification game is strong this week" not "Verification Behaviour: 8/10."

## Anti-references

- **Grafana/Datadog** — Not an infrastructure monitoring wall. No alert states, no 47-panel grid.
- **Generic SaaS dark dashboards** — Not the dark-bg + green-accent + radar-chart template. That's every AI tool landing page in 2025-2026.
- **Corporate HR/training dashboards** — Not a manager surveillance tool. This is personal.
- **Over-gamified (Duolingo owl)** — Motivating but not infantilizing. No mascots, no fake urgency streaks.

## Design Principles

1. **Post-run summary** — The dashboard is one screen you glance at for 10 seconds. Lead with the insight, not the data.
2. **Athletic, not corporate** — Use the language of personal performance. Effort, progress, personal bests. Not KPIs and metrics.
3. **Honest coaching** — Don't soften weak scores. A weak area should feel like something to work on, not something to feel bad about.
4. **Developer identity** — The craft and density of a tool made by developers for developers. Monospace where data lives. No wasted pixels, but breathing room where it serves clarity.
5. **Celebrate momentum** — Show progress and improvement. Make getting better feel tangible.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. 4.5:1 contrast minimum for all text. Keyboard navigable. Screen reader friendly with semantic HTML. prefers-reduced-motion respected. prefers-color-scheme respected.
